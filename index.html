Glass Guide

Your pub glassware cheat sheet

Pint Glass

Volume: 568 ml (UK) / 473 ml (US)

Used for lagers, IPAs, and stouts. The nonic pint is common in the UK, while the shaker pint is typical in the US.

Wine Glass

Volume: 350–450 ml

Designed to enhance aroma and flavor. Red wines prefer wider bowls, while whites are served in narrower glasses.

Rocks Glass

Volume: 180–300 ml

Short and stout, perfect for spirits served neat or on the rocks like whiskey or an Old Fashioned.

Snifter

Volume: 300–500 ml

Used for brandy or cognac. Its shape concentrates aromas and warms the liquid with your hand.

Highball Glass

Volume: 240–350 ml

Tall and narrow, ideal for mixed drinks like gin and tonic, rum and coke, or highballs.

Cocktail Glass

Volume: 90–300 ml

Iconic V-shape for martinis and other shaken or stirred drinks. A symbol of elegance and sophistication.
